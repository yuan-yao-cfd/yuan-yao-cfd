A facile microfluidic strategy for measuing interfacial tension

H Zhou, Y Yao, Q Chen, G Li, S Yao

Abstract

We report a facile method for measuring interfacial tension (IFT, c) of immiscible fluids using a microfluidic device. The IFT is determined by monitoring the pressure drop across a microchannel, where a pair of modified Laplace sensors (formed by tapered channels) are connected, and the curvatures of the interfaces in the tapered channels. The method was tested with the model oil/water systems, and the results agreed well with a commercial tensiometry. We expect this method to be easily implemented in common microfluidic laboratories and supply a low-cost and fast way for interfacial tension measurement.
